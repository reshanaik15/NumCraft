Factorial
Factorial is the operation of multiplying a number by all the positive integers smaller than it:
Definition: Factorial is the product of all positive integers from 1 to a given number. It’s used in permutations, combinations, and many areas of mathematics.
Symbol: The symbol for factorial is an exclamation mark (!).
Result: The result of the factorial operation is the total product of the number and all smaller positive integers.
Explanation: Factorial can be understood as the total number of ways to arrange or order a set of items. For example, 5! (5 factorial) means 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1, which equals 120.
